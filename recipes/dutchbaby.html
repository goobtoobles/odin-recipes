Dutch Baby

Description

A Dutch Baby is a large, eggy pancake that you bake in the oven. It is typically a breakfast food, although having it for brunch is ok, too. Some people call it a Bismarck, but I don't. I think it's way more fun to say "I'm hungry for a Dutch Baby".

While it would be pretty good without any accoutrements, I highly recommend them. Anything you'd put on a pancake works here, but my favorite is a squeeze of lemon and some powdered sugar. Jam is pretty great, too.

Another option is to make a savory version, if that's your thing. Just add a little ground black pepper and some herbs or somesuch to the batter. I like this version with ham and greens. Either way, slice it up like a pie to serve. It would be a bit much to eat the whole baby.

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 4 tbps butter

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350F
  2. Measure the flour into a small mixing bowl
  3. Add the eggs and the milk to the flour
  4. Mix into a nice, smooth batter
  5. Place the butter into a 10" cast iron skillet (double the recipe if you have a 12" skillet)
  6. Heat the skillet until the butter begins to foam
  7. Pour the batter into the skillet
  8. Bake for 30 minutes
  9. Top as you please
